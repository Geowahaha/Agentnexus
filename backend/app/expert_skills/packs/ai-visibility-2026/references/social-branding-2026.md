# "If I launch a new social/brand account TODAY" — 2026 Algorithm Rules

Use for new brand accounts or social strategy. Core 2026 shift: social platforms are now (a) AI training/answer sources, (b) search engines in their own right, and (c) entity-verification signals for E-E-A-T. A brand's social graph feeds its AI citability.

## Universal rules (all platforms)
1. **Entity consistency first:** same handle, name, bio facts, NAP, and link across every platform; all profiles listed in the website's `sameAs` schema. AI systems cross-verify identity — mismatches lower trust everywhere.
2. **Native > links:** every algorithm suppresses outbound links in primary content; deliver value in-platform, link in comments/bio/stories.
3. **Watch-time & saves beat likes:** rankers optimize retention (3-sec hold, completion rate) and "meaningful" actions (saves, shares, replies). Design the first 1–3 seconds as the hook; design content people save (checklists, specs, price guides).
4. **Reply speed is a ranking factor** (and a conversion one): platforms reward accounts that respond fast; pin a response-time promise.
5. **Search-optimize inside platforms:** captions/titles/on-screen text carry keywords — TikTok/IG/YouTube are where under-35 buyers search; their content also surfaces in Google and AI answers.
6. **Consistency over volume:** sustainable cadence (e.g., 3 quality posts/week) outperforms bursts; algorithms model account reliability.
7. **Proof content wins:** behind-the-scenes process, real results, named humans. Same E-E-A-T logic as the web — first-hand experience is the differentiator AI and audiences both reward.

## Platform notes (2026)
- **Facebook:** organic page reach ~1–6%; Meaningful Social Interactions reward conversation-starting posts, native video (esp. Reels), and fast replies. Groups and Messenger/LINE-style direct channels carry the actual relationship. For Thai SMEs, Facebook remains the discovery surface; pair with LINE OA as the conversion/CRM channel (rich menu, auto-reply, broadcast — where the quote actually happens).
- **Instagram:** Reels + carousels (saves); SEO-keyword captions; alt text on images.
- **TikTok:** search-intent content ("how to choose X") has long shelf life; completion rate dominates; reply-with-video to comments compounds reach.
- **YouTube:** the second-largest search engine and a primary AI-answer source; long-form how-tos + Shorts funnels; chapters and transcripts make content extractable (and citable).
- **LinkedIn:** for B2B (e.g., industrial casting buyers): founder/engineer personal accounts outperform company pages; document-format posts (carousels) and comment-led distribution.
- **X:** real-time + Grok visibility; threads with concrete data get quoted.

## Launch sequence for a new brand account
1. Register the same handle everywhere (even unused platforms — entity squatting protection).
2. Complete EVERY profile field; bio = who/what/where/proof + one CTA link (or link-in-bio page on your own domain).
3. Add all profiles to website `sameAs` schema + footer; link back from each profile to the site (entity loop closed).
4. Publish 9–12 foundation posts before promotion: 3 proof (process/results), 3 answer-content (top buyer questions — recycled from website answer blocks), 3 people (founder, team, customers).
5. Pick ONE primary platform by audience (Thai local B2C: Facebook+LINE; B2B export: LinkedIn+YouTube) — win one before adding more.
6. Measure: saves, shares, profile→site clicks, DM/LINE conversations started — not follower count. Tie to the website citation probes: brands with active social entities get named more in AI answers.

## Social ↔ AI visibility loop
Answer blocks on the site become carousel/video scripts; social proof (reviews, UGC, named customers) becomes website E-E-A-T evidence; consistent entity across both is what lets AI engines confidently say "this business exists, is reputable, and is located here." Audit both together, always.
